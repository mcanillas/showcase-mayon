Brief History of Mayon Volcano

Did you know? Mayon Volcano is renowned for its almost perfect conical shape and is considered one of the world's most active volcanoes.

Origins and Formation

Mayon Volcano, standing at 2,463 meters above sea level, is located in the province of Albay in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. The volcano's formation began approximately 20,000 years ago through successive eruptions that built up its iconic symmetrical cone shape.

Historical Eruptions

Mayon has erupted over 50 times in recorded history, making it the most active volcano in the Philippines. The first recorded eruption dates back to 1616.

Notable Eruptions

1814 Eruption

The most devastating eruption in Mayon's history. It buried the town of Cagsawa, killing over 1,200 people. The Cagsawa Church ruins remain as a memorial and popular tourist destination today.

1897 Eruption

Claimed approximately 400 lives and caused significant destruction to surrounding areas.

1993 Eruption

Resulted in 75 fatalities, including several farmers and scientists who were caught by pyroclastic flows.

2018 Eruption

One of the most recent major eruptions, prompting the evacuation of over 80,000 residents. The eruption lasted several weeks with continuous lava fountaining and ash emissions.

The Legend of Daragang Magayon

According to local folklore, the volcano is named after the beautiful maiden Daragang Magayon (meaning "beautiful maiden" in Bicolano). Legend tells of her tragic love story with the warrior Panganoran, whose deaths led to the formation of the volcano. This romantic tale has become deeply embedded in Bicolano culture and identity.

Modern Monitoring and Safety

Today, Mayon Volcano is closely monitored by the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS). The agency maintains a permanent danger zone of 6-8 kilometers radius around the volcano's summit, which is strictly off-limits to the public.

Despite its dangers, Mayon remains a source of pride for Filipinos, particularly for the people of Albay. Its perfect cone continues to attract tourists, scientists, and adventurers from around the world.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Albay Province, Bicol Region, Philippines
  • Height: 2,463 meters (8,081 feet)
  • Type: Stratovolcano
  • Number of Historical Eruptions: Over 50
  • Most Destructive Eruption: 1814
  • Most Recent Major Eruption: 2018
